## 2024-04-08 - Email Case-Sensitivity and Prisma findUnique
**Vulnerability:** Registration and login endpoints did not normalize email casing (e.g., `user@example.com` vs `User@example.com`), allowing duplicate account creation and potential authentication bypasses or lockouts.
**Learning:** Fixing this requires normalizing emails (`trim().toLowerCase()`). However, Prisma's `findUnique` does not support `mode: 'insensitive'`, which is needed to prevent locking out existing users who registered with uppercase letters. Switching to `findFirst` is required, which in turn necessitates updating all associated test mocks (`findUnique` to `findFirst`) across Jest and Bun test suites.
**Prevention:** Always normalize email inputs at the boundaries of the application (registration, login, profile updates) and ensure database queries handling these inputs are case-insensitive or that the database column itself enforces case-insensitivity. When updating Prisma queries, ensure corresponding test mocks are also updated to prevent CI failures.
